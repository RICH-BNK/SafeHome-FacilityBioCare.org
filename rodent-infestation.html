5 Signs You May Have a Rodent Infestation

March 15, 2026 | 4 min read

Rodents are masters of hiding. By the time you see a rat or mouse in broad daylight, the infestation is already significant. Learning the early warning signs can save you thousands in property damage and protect your family from dangerous diseases.

1. Droppings – The Most Obvious Sign

Rodent droppings are typically found near food sources, along walls, or inside cabinets. Mouse droppings are small (3-6mm) and pointed; rat droppings are larger (12-18mm) and banana-shaped. Fresh droppings are dark and moist; old ones are gray and crumble easily. If you see droppings, you have an active infestation.

2. Gnaw Marks on Furniture, Wires, and Walls

Rats and mice constantly gnaw to keep their teeth from overgrowing. Look for fresh wood shavings, chewed electrical wires (a fire hazard), and holes in baseboards, boxes, or food packaging. Rodent teeth leave rough, irregular marks (unlike smooth cuts from insects).

3. Strange Noises in Walls or Ceilings

Rodents are nocturnal. If you hear scratching, scampering, or squeaking sounds at night coming from inside walls, attics, or under floors – that’s a classic sign. Mice are light and fast; rats are heavier and produce louder thumping sounds.

4. Grease Marks and Rub Marks

As rodents travel along the same paths repeatedly, their oily fur leaves dark, greasy smudges on walls, pipes, and baseboards. These rub marks are often found near entry holes or along floorboards. In dusty areas, you may also see tail drag marks.

5. Nests and Shredded Materials

Rodents build nests using shredded paper, fabric, insulation, and dried plants. Check dark, secluded areas like attics, storage boxes, behind appliances, and under furniture. A nest indicates a breeding population – the problem is already serious.
